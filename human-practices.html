Integrated Human Practices

How our project interacts with society—and how society shapes our project

Our project is not developed in isolation. Through continuous engagement with stakeholders, experts, and the public, we ensure that our work is socially responsible, ethically grounded, and aligned with real-world needs.

Stakeholder Engagement

We conducted interviews with clinicians, researchers, and affected individuals to better understand the challenges surrounding PCOS and current treatment limitations.

"This approach could offer a more targeted alternative to current therapies, but safety and controllability are key."

- Clinical Expert

Based on this feedback, we refined our design to prioritize safety mechanisms and controllable gene expression.

Design Integration

Insights from our stakeholders directly influenced our project decisions:

  • Incorporation of safety switches in our genetic circuit
  • Focus on clinically relevant delivery methods
  • Consideration of patient accessibility and usability

Public Engagement & Education

We believe that science should be accessible to everyone. Our team engaged with the public through outreach activities designed to demystify synthetic biology.

🏫 School Outreach

Introduced high school students to synthetic biology concepts.

🧠 Workshops

Organized interactive sessions on ethics and biotechnology.

🌐 Online Content

Created accessible educational materials for a broader audience.

Ethics & Responsibility

Developing a live-cell therapeutic raises important ethical considerations, including safety, long-term effects, and accessibility.

We addressed these concerns by:

  • Designing biological containment strategies
  • Evaluating potential risks and mitigation strategies
  • Engaging with experts in bioethics

Reflection

Integrated Human Practices is an ongoing process. As our project evolves, we continuously incorporate new perspectives to improve both the science and its societal impact.
